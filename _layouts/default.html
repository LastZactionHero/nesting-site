Your Business with a Baby & Beyond

Did you start your business in order to have more time and flexibility with your future family?

Good for you.

Whether you’re a designer, copywriter, coach, blogger, in ecommerce or another field...the Internet has allowed moms to rewrite the rules on when, where, and how they work to create a lifestyle that works for them.

You’re a pregnant business owner - Congratulations! First, on your upcoming bundle of joy and Second, on entering this tribe of women changing how we work so we can be there for our kids.

You’re about to use that freedom for the first time to...
...design a maternity leave that is completely unique
to you, your business, and your new family.

You have the freedom to decide exactly what you want to do.

You’re not in a corporate job that dictates how much leave you can take and how much you can make while you’re recovering and getting to know your new baby.



Will you be working at all in the first few weeks or months?

Will you hire a team to keep the lights on?

Will you have a passive product running to bring in some income?

Will you keep your current clients or offboard them?

All this freedom is empowering. It’s also overwhelming.
Especially for your pregnant body and mind.

I know because I’ve been there.

My Story

I’m an entrepreneur because I want a family centric lifestyle alongside a fulfilling career and I’m committed to helping other families navigate this path for themselves.

  • I left the corporate world because I just couldn’t see dropping a 12-week-old at daycare five days a week.
  • I took my skills and discovered the online solopreneur world where I provided COO-for-hire services - digital marketing projects and launches, virtual team management, and strategic business organization/efficiency.
  • Pregnant with our son, I had a long list of projects to complete to create the perfect paid maternity leave.
  • I struggled to get my plans in place and realized just how much pregnancy can affect how you work and how hard it is to take on a huge project all by yourself.
  • I started Nesting Your Business to help entrepreneurs get organized and strategic about their business at this very important stage for soon-to-be-moms.

I know the struggle and I’m here to lend a strategic mind and motivated soul to ensure you get the maternity leave you deserve, your way.

I started my business so that I could have the lifestyle I wanted with my kids. It was the whole reason I quit my corporate job. I just didn’t want to drop a 12-week-old at daycare five days a week.

When I first got started I didn’t have my son, so I built my business the way I knew how to work. I worked a typical 9-5 day, I responded to emails within minutes, and provided entrepreneurs with COO-for-hire services. I managed digital marketing projects and launches, motivated teams and got businesses organized and running efficiently. I was available all the time, and my clients loved it.

X Read More X

Flash forward 2 years and I’m pregnant with our son. I had a lot of ideas about the kind of maternity leave I wanted to take and how I’d set up my business to work during this time.

Now know I’m a project manager. I had a list of objectives for my business and a hard deadline (9 months!). This should have been my bread and butter...but I couldn’t get the work done. I couldn’t believe it. I’d always been the kind of person that made a goal, wrote out a plan of attack, and got to work. My business was built on this very talent.

During pregnancy, I had too many days where I felt scattered, tired, and my motivation was so low I just wished someone would give me a simple list of tasks to accomplish each day. I realized just how much pregnancy affects how you work and how hard it is to take on a project - like designing your maternity leave - all by yourself.

How We Work Together

At the end of our time together your business will be a well-oiled machine. The machine you’ve probably been dreaming about since long before you got pregnant.

Strategy

We’ll start with a Strategy Call. We’ll get to know each other and I’ll learn all about your business and your goals for your maternity leave. We’ll review options you’ve considered and those you may not have thought of. We’ll talk about any hang-ups you’ve had with getting work done during pregnancy.
